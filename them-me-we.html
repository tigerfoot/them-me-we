cat ME

An interest
is
born

In 1998, after being certified Microsoft Engineer since 4 years, and doing consulting in computer's services as freelancer.
We (mean Françoise and Myself) decided that the time to create a common consulting enterprise had come.
We founded Ioda-Net Sàrl at that time, located in Switzerland, not that far from Basel.

Free software?

At that time we needed a bunch of software for our servers.
Be it script languages, databases engine, network management and so on.
I met then those groups of people who were proposing free and open source software.

Legal!

If you run an enterprise, you have to respect the law. There's no alternative.
As a small start-up, we didn't have a big budget to spent on licenses or fees.
Using free software in our case help us to build the business.

THEY offer

Me use

My most important need was covered.
Me become a free software user.
And it didn't took that much time before I became an advocate.
In a sense this was the first form of giving back to the community.

KEY

Determine YOUR primary need,
 
Find YOUR way to fill it.
 
Start with one!

You certainly have a special interest on a particular domain. Be it related to your job, or as a hobby.
I wouldn't be surprised if some other share the same in the free world. Try to find them, and start to learn how to contribute and interact with.
At the beginning, try as much as you can to concentrate your effort on only one subject.
The world is so big, you could loose yourself and your motivation if you act like a bee.
Trust me! ;-)

THEM?

Do they know there's a problem?

Will they resolve it?

Around 2005, with a Debian distribution, absolute reference for servers at that time, I encountered my first clash.
Inside the delivered pure-ftpd package there was a missing compilation flag. (--enable large-file)
I opened a bug, and this one got a WONTFIX answer :
Quote: there's no reason to have support for file over 4GB. End Quote
I had seen one, delivering the first DVD iso to end users :-)
But Debian's rules were clear, maintainer is god.
Over!

Of course, yes we can.
But honestly there's a time when, if you have to recompile more than half of what you need.
You start to dream of greener grass fields.

Moving, Jumping....
This reaction is quite typical of new comers inside the free and open source world.
They will jump from one project or distribution to another one every time they will find an "unsolvable" bug.
And we certainly done it like this, and do it again any time we discover a new project.
But honestly nothing is giving better results than fixing things by ourselves.

Real coders behind this feature

If you're like me, you behave like a geek, don't you?
Yesterday night, only a small update was proposed. Nothing to really worry about, right?
But then this morning, it's a no mail today show!
Damn, and the customer is waiting for that urgent mail, right now.
ME is in crisis mode. KMAIL2 what a fuck.
I can't believe there was someone angriest than me this fateful day.
Well until ...

THEM

one

Until Myriam Schweingruber (well known as Mamarok) told me that behind kmail2 there's mostly only one developer. His name Laurent Montel.
Well It's THEM without the M!!!
Me twist immediately my mind. The WE part take again priority 0.
How can I help him?
Stop whining, if there's a bug, do your best to reproduce it, get a back-trace, and communicate with all parties involved like openSUSE packagers, upstream developer and so on.
And also learn (again!!!) to split problem which are typically kmail's from those related to akonadi.

WE + WE = WE (*)

PostgreSQL

(*) What other result did you expect?

I would like to add another story, when several communities helped each others.
On one side, one postgresql's expert named Cédric Villemain. On the other side QT's developpers. Cédric drill down the QT code to explain how to do "string escaping" in sql statement, the way it should.
It took time, a few weeks in reality, until each side recognized the expertise of the other one.
But finally the bug was closed and a fix made.

WE x WE = WE (*)

(*) This is not maths, listen the talker :-)


Now to drive the nail, about the strength of free and open source model. I can't hide this story.
With irc, the main developer, good relationship, obtaining a vital patch to fix nasty segfault of mapserver.
In seventeens minutes, customer production can run again.
At that time, the customer had also understood our forces.
